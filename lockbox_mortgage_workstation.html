Lead to lock — sequence
1

Same-day response with full scenario

Current loan recap, new payment, LTV improvement, monthly savings, and the no-cash-due path — all in the first reply. Use the Email Generator tab.

2

Separate escrows from closing costs in every quote

Escrows aren't a fee — they're the client's own money going into their new account. They get the old balance back within 20 days. Show this proactively so the gross number never lands as a shock.

3

Issue disclosures the same hour credit is authorized

Don't let a business day pass between "yes, pull my credit" and the portal invite.

4

Proactively offer the no-cash-due structure

Roll escrows, skip the next payment. Remove the biggest friction point before they bring it up.

5

Match any competitor quote immediately

Never let a competitor offer sit overnight. Match the rate, then use breakeven math to reframe why your structure wins. See Option Builder.

6

Deliver the side-by-side table within the hour

When a client asks to see both options, the comparison is what closes them. Use Option Builder → copy the table into your reply.

7

One ask per email — always end with a single next step

Multiple asks = no action. Sign disclosures. Confirm income. Pick a rate. One thing per message.

8

Lock with a 15-day window, framed as protecting gains

"We've already seen significant improvement — locking protects that." Urgency without pressure.
