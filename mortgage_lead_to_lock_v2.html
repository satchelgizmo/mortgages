Common objections

"I got a lower rate from another lender."

Match it immediately, then reframe mathematically. Calculate the breakeven on any point or cost difference. Most clients refi again in 3–4 years — day-one savings in your pocket usually wins over a marginally lower payment.

"Should I wait for rates to drop more?"

Quantify what they're leaving on the table each month they wait. "Lock your savings today — when we hit your target rate, we'll refi again." Show that the market moved in their favor already and could reverse.

"I'm not sure about the closing costs."

Separate loan costs from escrows. Escrows aren't a fee — they're their own money going into their own account, and they'll get the old escrow back within 20 days. Net cost is often far lower than the headline number suggests.

"Can you send me a breakdown in writing?"

Send the side-by-side table within the hour. Never make them ask twice. Use the Option Builder tab → paste directly into your email reply.

"I want to think about it."

Create urgency without pressure: "Totally fair — just note this rate is available for 15 days and markets can move. Happy to hold while you decide." Follow up in 24 hrs.

"What if the appraisal comes in low?"

Lead with the appraisal waiver. If not available, set conservative expectations early — a pleasant surprise beats a revised disclosure that shakes confidence.

Lead to lock — step by step

1

Same-day response with full scenario

Don't just say yes — send current loan recap, new payment, monthly savings, LTV improvement, and the no-cash-due option. Use the Email Generator tab.

2

Separate escrows from closing costs in every quote

Escrows are not a fee. Show them gross cash to close, then break out escrows vs. loan costs vs. lender credit so the net number is clear and not alarming.

3

Pull credit & issue disclosures immediately

As soon as income confirmed and credit authorized, the portal invite goes out the same hour. Don't let a day pass.

4

Proactively offer no-cash-due structure

Before they ask, show how rolling escrows + skipping next payment = $0 due at closing. Remove the biggest friction point on your terms.

5

Match any competitor rate immediately

Never let a competitor quote sit overnight. Match and reframe with breakeven math. Use the Objection Handling tab.

6

Send the side-by-side table on request — fast

When they ask to "see both options," respond within the hour. Use the Option Builder tab. The table in this thread is what got her to lock.

7

One ask per email, end every message with a clear next step

Multiple asks = no action. Each email ends with exactly one specific thing: sign disclosures, confirm income, pick a rate.

8

Lock with a 15-day window, frame it as protecting gains

"We've already seen significant improvement — locking now protects that." Urgency without pressure.
